Species Spotlight:

Lavender

There are a lot of different types of Lavenders and they grow across different continents of the world. There are about 39 different species known. All Lavenders belong to the genus 'Lavandula' and are in the plant family known as Labiatae. This plant family also includes other garden herb plants such as Thymes, Mints, Rosemary, Basil, Sage and Savory. These herbs share some characteristics in common. They all have Lavender flowers that are "lipped" in form which is why the plant family name of Labiatae or the newly accepted botanical term Lamiaceae. Their stems when cut cross-wise are square in appearance. Lastly, these Lavender plants are all aromatic and fragrant and can be used to make distilled Lavender essential oil.
